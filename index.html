Nikku's Cheat Sheet

neighborhood-by-neighborhood notes, for whenever you want to improvise

🧣 West Village

Cutesy daytime wandering — Cornelia St, Pop-Up Bagels, PopUp Grocer, and chilling in Washington Square Park (surrounded by NYU/college energy + cute coffee spots).

McDougal & Bleecker St are the touristy main drags — that's where Comedy Cellar is. Buy tickets online ahead; famous comedians do surprise drop-ins (Nikku saw Hasan Minhaj there!).

Froyo: Culture, Birdies, Myka
Drink: Dante's — espresso martinis are a MUST, especially outside
Pizza tour: L'Industrie, Mama's Too, Bleecker St Pizza, Joe's Pizza

🌇 Chelsea / Meatpacking

Walk the West Side Highway over to Little Island, then Chelsea Market and the High Line. A great half-day stroll with snacks and views along the way.

🛍️ SoHo

The shopping neighborhood — flagship stores, boutiques, and people-watching along Broadway and the side streets.

🥐 East Village

Fun dinner spots and bars — very "twenties in NYC" if you bop around at night. Bakery-dense by day too.

Bagels: Apollo Bagels
Bakeries: Sunday Morning (cinnamon rolls), Hani's Bakery, Librae Bakery, La Cabra
Coffee: Hidden Grounds (Indian-themed — Nikku's favorite!)
Vibes: Book Club Bar, Kolkata Chai Co

🍷 Lower East Side

Bars popular with the younger 20s crowd. A wine happy hour at sunset is a must — Orchard Street closes to cars and gets very vibey. Get there early for an outdoor table.

Don't miss: Skin Contact (or really anywhere on Orchard St)

🏠 Brooklyn (home base)

The Everly, 180 Ashland Place, Apt 3704 — landing pad for jet lag naps, late nights, and easy mornings before heading into Manhattan.

You're staying right in Fort Greene — one of the best neighborhoods in Brooklyn! Gorgeous brownstone streets, Fort Greene Park for picnicking and skyline views, and a ton of great food and coffee within a few blocks. So so good.
